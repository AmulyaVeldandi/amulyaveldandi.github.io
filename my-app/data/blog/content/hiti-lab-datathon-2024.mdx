## Event Overview

Building on the foundation from 2023, the HITI Lab Datathon 2024 pushed participants to new frontiers in healthcare AI. This year's focus shifted towards clinical natural language processing and decision support systems, addressing the critical need for automated clinical documentation and intelligent patient risk stratification.

## Challenge: Clinical NLP & Risk Prediction

### Problem Statement

Healthcare systems generate massive amounts of unstructured clinical text—radiology reports, clinical notes, discharge summaries—that contain crucial patient information but remain largely untapped for decision support. Our challenge:

**Build an NLP pipeline to extract structured insights from clinical narratives and predict patient outcomes**

### Dataset

- **Size**: 50,000+ de-identified clinical notes
- **Sources**: Emergency department notes, radiology reports, discharge summaries
- **Annotations**: Limited ground truth for key clinical entities
- **Task**: Extract clinical concepts, relationships, and predict 30-day readmission risk

## Our Solution Architecture

### Phase 1: Clinical Entity Recognition

**Approach**: Fine-tuned BioClinicalBERT for medical entity extraction

```python
# Key entities targeted
- Medications and dosages
- Diagnoses and conditions
- Procedures and interventions
- Lab results and vital signs
- Social determinants of health
```

**Challenges Addressed**:
- Handling medical abbreviations and acronyms
- Dealing with negation and uncertainty
- Extracting temporal information
- Managing context dependencies

### Phase 2: Relationship Extraction

Built a relationship extraction module to connect entities:

1. **Medication-Condition Links**: Which drugs treat which conditions
2. **Temporal Sequences**: Order of events and procedures
3. **Causal Relationships**: Cause-effect patterns in clinical narratives
4. **Severity Indicators**: Extracting severity modifiers and qualifiers

### Phase 3: Risk Prediction Model

**Multi-modal Architecture**:
- **Text Encoder**: BERT-based representations of clinical notes
- **Structured Data**: Demographics, lab values, vital signs
- **Fusion Layer**: Attention-based combination of modalities
- **Output**: Probability of 30-day readmission

**Key Innovation**: Attention mechanism that highlighted which parts of clinical notes contributed most to predictions, providing interpretability for clinicians.

## Technical Implementation

### NLP Pipeline

**Preprocessing**:
```
Clinical Text → Tokenization → Entity Recognition →
Relationship Extraction → Feature Engineering → Risk Scoring
```

**Libraries & Frameworks**:
- **Transformers**: Hugging Face for BERT models
- **spaCy**: Medical entity recognition and custom pipelines
- **scispaCy**: Biomedical NLP components
- **BioBERT**: Pre-trained medical language models
- **UMLS**: Unified Medical Language System for concept normalization

### Model Training Strategy

1. **Transfer Learning**: Started with BioClinicalBERT pre-trained on MIMIC-III
2. **Domain Adaptation**: Fine-tuned on provided clinical notes
3. **Multi-task Learning**: Jointly trained entity recognition and relation extraction
4. **Ensemble**: Combined multiple model predictions for robustness

### Evaluation Framework

**Metrics**:
- **Entity Recognition**: F1-score of 0.89 for key medical entities
- **Readmission Prediction**: AUC-ROC of 0.82, precision-recall AUPRC of 0.76
- **Calibration**: Brier score of 0.14, indicating well-calibrated probabilities
- **Clinical Validation**: 91% agreement with clinician assessments on test cases

## Lessons Learned

### Technical Insights

1. **Data Quality > Model Complexity**: Clean, well-annotated data outperformed complex models on noisy data
2. **Domain Pre-training Matters**: BioClinicalBERT significantly outperformed general BERT
3. **Calibration is Critical**: Well-calibrated probabilities more useful than raw predictions
4. **Inference Speed**: Real-time constraints shaped architecture choices

### Process Improvements

1. **Version Control Everything**: Data splits, model checkpoints, evaluation scripts
2. **Continuous Validation**: Frequent check-ins with clinicians prevented wasted effort
3. **Modular Design**: Separated components enabled parallel development
4. **Documentation**: Clear READMEs and comments saved time during debugging

### Collaborative Best Practices

1. **Shared Vocabulary**: Established common terminology early
2. **Role Clarity**: Everyone knew their responsibilities
3. **Decision Framework**: Clear process for resolving disagreements
4. **Celebration Moments**: Recognized small wins to maintain momentum
