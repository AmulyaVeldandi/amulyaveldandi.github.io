## INDIANAPOLIS, IN — January 2025

Throughout my Master's program in Health Informatics at Indiana University, I have been honored to receive five competitive scholarships and grants totaling significant financial support. These awards have enabled me to focus on academic excellence, attend premier conferences, and contribute to the healthcare informatics community.

---

## SIIM NIIC Alumni Scholarship (May 2025)

![SIIM NIIC Alumni Scholarship Award](/images/scholarships/siim-niic-2025.jpg)

The [Society for Imaging Informatics in Medicine (SIIM)](https://www.siim.org/) awarded me the prestigious NIIC Alumni Scholarship in May 2025. This competitive scholarship recognizes students who demonstrate exceptional promise in imaging informatics and commitment to advancing the field. The National Institute of Imaging Informatics (NIIC) provides world-class training in PACS integration, AI deployment in radiology workflows, and clinical imaging systems. This scholarship supported my attendance at the SIIM 2025 Annual Meeting in Nashville, Tennessee, where I connected with leading radiologists, informaticists, and AI engineers working on cutting-edge imaging solutions.

---

## Women & Hi-Tech SIM Indy Leading the Future Scholarship (October 2024)

![Women & Hi-Tech Leading the Future Scholarship](/images/scholarships/women-hitech-2024.jpg)

In October 2024, I was selected for the [Women & Hi-Tech](https://www.womenhitech.org/) SIM Indy Leading the Future Scholarship. This award supports women pursuing careers in technology and informatics, with emphasis on leadership potential and commitment to advancing women in STEM fields. Women & Hi-Tech is a nonprofit organization dedicated to empowering women in technology through networking, mentorship, and professional development opportunities. The scholarship, awarded in partnership with the Society for Information Management (SIM) Indianapolis chapter, provided access to industry networking events, leadership workshops, and mentorship from senior women leaders in health IT.

---

## Ruth Walker Health Informatics Scholarship (June 2024)

The Ruth Walker Health Informatics Scholarship, awarded by Indiana University School of Informatics and Computing in June 2024, honors the legacy of Ruth Walker, a pioneering figure in health informatics education and practice. This merit-based scholarship recognizes students demonstrating academic excellence, research potential, and dedication to improving healthcare through informatics. The award is presented to students who exemplify the values Ruth Walker championed: integrating information technology into healthcare to improve patient outcomes and advancing the field through rigorous research and community engagement.

---

## Biohealth Informatics Research Travel Grant (April 2024)

In April 2024, I received a competitive Research Travel Grant from the Indiana University Luddy School of Informatics, Computing, and Engineering. This grant supported my attendance and presentation at the IEEE International Symposium on Biomedical Imaging (ISBI) 2024 in Athens, Greece. The grant covered conference registration, international airfare, accommodation, and related expenses, enabling me to present my research to leading scientists in biomedical imaging and machine learning. ISBI is the premier international forum for the presentation of technological advances in theoretical and applied biomedical imaging.

---

## IUI Luddy MS Student Scholarship (January 2023 – December 2024)

The Indiana University Luddy School of Informatics, Computing, and Engineering awarded me a two-year merit-based MS Student Scholarship covering January 2023 through December 2024. This foundational scholarship provided significant tuition support throughout my Master's program in Health Informatics. The renewable award is granted to incoming graduate students who demonstrate strong academic credentials, research potential, and commitment to the field of informatics. This scholarship enabled me to focus on coursework in machine learning, clinical decision support, health data analytics, and biomedical informatics while maintaining academic excellence.

---

## Impact and Gratitude

These five scholarships and grants represent more than financial support—they are investments in the future of health informatics and recognition of the importance of bringing diverse perspectives to healthcare technology. I am deeply grateful to SIIM, Women & Hi-Tech, Indiana University, and all the scholarship committees and donors who made these awards possible.

The support I received enabled me to pursue rigorous academic study, attend leading conferences, build professional networks, and contribute to research advancing the field. I am committed to paying this forward by mentoring future students, contributing to the health informatics community, and developing AI solutions that improve healthcare for all.

## About the Organizations

**Society for Imaging Informatics in Medicine (SIIM)**
[siim.org](https://www.siim.org/)
SIIM is the leading organization for professionals working at the intersection of imaging and informatics, with membership including radiologists, imaging informaticists, administrators, IT professionals, physicists, and industry partners.

**Women & Hi-Tech**
[womenhitech.org](https://www.womenhitech.org/)
Women & Hi-Tech is a nonprofit organization empowering women in technology through community, mentorship, and professional development. The organization serves the greater Indianapolis area and beyond, supporting women at all career stages in technology fields.

**Indiana University Luddy School of Informatics, Computing, and Engineering**
The Luddy School is one of the nation's premier informatics programs, offering cutting-edge education in health informatics, data science, and computing at Indiana University's Indianapolis campus.
